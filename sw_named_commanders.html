1. How Commanders Work
Named commanders are individuals with enough personal authority, skill, or reputation to meaningfully change the outcome of battles and campaign actions. They are not abstract bonuses — they are real people who can be in only one place at a time, can be killed, and whose presence or absence changes everything around them.

Most of the galaxy runs on Status Quo. Moffs administrate. Admirals patrol. Planetary Governors collect taxes. You only interact with the commander system when you choose to deploy a named commander, when a named commander is threatened, or when a battle forces a casualty roll.
RuleDetail
One location at a timeA commander can only be in one sector at once. They cannot affect two battles in the same turn.
Movement costs a turnMoving a commander from one sector to another uses their entire turn. They cannot perform their specialty action the same turn they move.
Status Quo defaultCommanders not given specific orders remain at their current location performing their baseline duties. You only act when changing what they do.
Specialty once per turnEach commander can use their specialty effect once per turn while present in a sector.
Casualty roll after battleAny named commander present during a battle rolls on the casualty table after the battle resolves. Higher command ratings have better survival odds.
Morale modifierA named commander present shifts the force's morale state up one level (see Scripting Reference Section 8).
2. Command Rating System
Command Rating reflects a commander's overall military and political authority. It affects their survival odds on the casualty roll and the strength of their specialty effect.
RatingTierExamplesCasualty Roll Modifier
5Grand Moff / Grand Admiral / Dark LordTarkin, Thrawn, Vader+2 to survival roll
4Admiral / General / Alliance LeaderDodonna, Raddus, Romodi, Mon Mothma+1 to survival roll
3Sector Commander / Moff / Rebel Sector CommanderKonstantine, Pryce, Cham Syndulla, HeraNo modifier
2Field Commander / Cell LeaderRex, Sato, Sabine, Kallus-1 to survival roll
1Operative / Agent / IndependentEzra, Zeb, Saw Gerrera-2 to survival roll
Rating vs Danger
Higher rating does not mean safer. A Rating 5 commander who is present during a Total Loss battle still rolls on the casualty table — they just have better odds of surviving it. Vader at the front is still Vader at the front.
3. Movement Rules
SituationRule
Standard moveCommander moves from current sector to any adjacent sector. Costs their full turn action — no specialty use that turn.
Long-distance moveMoving more than one sector away takes 2 turns. Commander is in transit and unavailable for both turns.
Emergency deploymentA Rating 5 commander (Tarkin, Thrawn, Vader) can move any distance in 1 turn by invoking Imperial authority — but this costs 1 Political Will. Represents the resource expenditure of emergency hyperspace priority.
Rebel mobilityRebel commanders can move any distance in 1 turn using their clandestine transport networks — but the move must be declared secretly and revealed simultaneously with the Imperial turn.
Wounded commanderCannot move for 2 turns. Remains at current location. Can still perform specialty at -1 effectiveness.
Commander in transitCannot use specialty. Cannot be targeted by assassination. Cannot affect morale of any force.
4. Death / Wound / Capture
After any battle in which a named commander was present, roll 1d6 and apply the modifier based on their Command Rating. The battle outcome also affects the roll.
Battle Outcome Modifiers
Battle ResultRoll Modifier
Victory — Light losses (under 25%)+2 to roll
Victory — Moderate losses (25-49%)+1 to roll
Victory — Heavy losses (50-99%)No modifier
Defeat — managed retreat-1 to roll
Defeat — Heavy losses-2 to roll
Total Loss (force destroyed)-3 to roll
Casualty Roll Results (1d6 + modifiers)
1
Killed
Commander is dead. Removed permanently. Faction loses 1 Political Will.
2
Captured
Commander is captured by the enemy. Opponent gains 2 Political Will. Can be exchanged or rescued.
3
Wounded
Commander is wounded. Out of action for 2 turns. Command Rating -1 while recovering.
4
Shaken
Commander is shaken. Command Rating -1 for next battle only. Then returns to normal.
5
Unharmed
Commander survives without injury. No effect.
6+
Heroic
Commander not only survives but performs notably. +1 Political Will for their faction.
Canonical Deaths Override the Dice
If a canonical death is triggered by a specific event (Battle of Atollon kills Sato, Konstantine's recklessness gets him destroyed) — that death happens regardless of the dice roll. The dice only apply to non-canonical battle outcomes.
Captured Commanders
SituationRule
Rebel captures Imperial commanderRebels can interrogate for 1 free Intelligence action. Can offer exchange or hold indefinitely. Empire loses specialty of that commander until freed.
Empire captures Rebel commanderEmpire gains 1 free ISB sweep action. Commander is transported to Imperial detention. Rebels can attempt extraction mission (triggers Entry 005).
Prisoner exchangeBoth factions can agree to exchange captured commanders — 1 for 1. Both commanders return to active duty next turn.
ExecutionEither faction can execute a captured commander. Executing grants +1 Political Will but the opposing faction gains +2 Political Will from the outrage. Usually not worth it.

EXTREMIST TACTICS
Saw's Partisans hit hard — when they participate in a battle the Rebel force deals casualties as if one tier higher (a Raid deals Medium damage, a Medium assault deals Heavy). However Saw costs the Rebels 1 Political Will every time he participates in a battle, win or lose — his methods are too brutal for the Alliance's public image. Mon Mothma publicly distances the Alliance from Saw's methods. Saw is not officially under Alliance command — he cooperates when it suits him.
Note
Saw knows about the Geonosian genocide and is investigating the Death Star. He may share intelligence — or not — based on how the campaign has treated him.
