How Event Cards Work
When to Draw At the start of Phase 2 of every turn, draw one Event Card. Events are resolved immediately before either player takes actions. Once drawn, a card is removed from the deck permanently — each event happens only once per campaign.
Resources Cards reference two resources: Political Will (PW) — the faction's ability to sustain the war. Supply — the material capacity to field and reinforce forces. Supply affects force readiness and morale state. PW at zero ends the campaign.
Ground Forces vs Fleet When a card affects military strength it specifies either Ground Forces or Fleet Strength, not a generic pool. Ground Force losses mean specific units lose headcount. Fleet losses mean specific ship classes are reduced.
Mandatory Cards Cards marked MANDATORY trigger regardless of when they are drawn — their events happen immediately and supersede normal turn order.

1. How Commanders Work
Named commanders are individuals with enough personal authority, skill, or reputation to meaningfully change the outcome of battles and campaign actions. They are not abstract bonuses — they are real people who can be in only one place at a time, can be killed, and whose presence or absence changes everything around them.

Most of the galaxy runs on Status Quo. Moffs administrate. Admirals patrol. Planetary Governors collect taxes. You only interact with the commander system when you choose to deploy a named commander, when a named commander is threatened, or when a battle forces a casualty roll.
RuleDetail
One location at a timeA commander can only be in one sector at once. They cannot affect two battles in the same turn.
Movement costs a turnMoving a commander from one sector to another uses their entire turn. They cannot perform their specialty action the same turn they move.
Status Quo defaultCommanders not given specific orders remain at their current location performing their baseline duties. You only act when changing what they do.
Specialty once per turnEach commander can use their specialty effect once per turn while present in a sector.
Casualty roll after battleAny named commander present during a battle rolls on the casualty table after the battle resolves. Higher command ratings have better survival odds.
Morale modifierA named commander present shifts the force's morale state up one level (see Scripting Reference Section 8).
2. Command Rating System
Command Rating reflects a commander's overall military and political authority. It affects their survival odds on the casualty roll and the strength of their specialty effect.

Rating vs Danger
Higher rating does not mean safer. A Rating 5 commander who is present during a Total Loss battle still rolls on the casualty table — they just have better odds of surviving it. Vader at the front is still Vader at the front.

Standard moveCommander moves from current sector to any adjacent sector. Costs their full turn action — no specialty use that turn.
Long-distance moveMoving more than one sector away takes 2 turns. Commander is in transit and unavailable for both turns.
Emergency deploymentA Rating 5 commander (Tarkin, Thrawn, Vader) can move any distance in 1 turn by invoking Imperial authority — but this costs 1 Political Will. Represents the resource expenditure of emergency hyperspace priority.

Wounded commanderCannot move for 2 turns. Remains at current location. Can still perform specialty at -1 effectiveness.
Commander in transitCannot use specialty. Cannot be targeted by assassination. Cannot affect morale of any force.

1. What is a Sector
The galaxy is divided into 29 sectors across 7 regions. Each sector contains planets and force cards — ground forces and fleets assigned to that sector. Everything that happens in the campaign happens within or between sectors.
Sector Contents
Every sector in the Sector Command document contains:

  • Planets — with their control status, governor, and situation notes
  • Ground Force cards — armies, garrisons, rebel cells with headcount and composition
  • Fleet cards — ships assigned to that sector with individual ship counts
  • Supply status — Nominal, Reduced, or Critical — affects morale and capability
  • Control status — Imperial, Rebel, Contested, Neutral, or Hutt Space
Forces Stay in Their Sector
Forces do not move automatically. A fleet or ground force stays in its assigned sector until a player spends an Action Point to move it. Moving a force is a deliberate campaign decision — not a passive event.
2. Turn Structure
01
Strategic Planning
Both players secretly write their action declarations. No discussion. No reveals.
02
Event Card
Draw one event card from the deck. Resolve its effect immediately.
03
Action Resolution
Both players reveal their declarations simultaneously. Resolve all actions. Battles are noted for filming.
04
Cleanup
Update supply, Political Will, morale, sector control. Advance the turn counter.
Battles Are Not Watched Live
When a battle is triggered in Phase 3 both players note what is fighting where. The GM films the battle after the session ends in Men of War (ground) or AotR (space) and posts the video. Players watch the video and learn the outcome. The next session begins with the updated campaign state.
3. Declaring an Attack
What Goes in an Attack Declaration
During Phase 1 a player writes their attack declaration privately on the Turn Tracker. A complete declaration includes:

  • Action type — Raid, Full Assault, Planetary Assault, Fleet Engagement
  • Target sector — which sector is being attacked
  • Attacking forces — which force cards are committing
  • Commander — which named commander if any is participating
  • Ground transport — if bringing ground forces, which fleet has the Gozantis or GR-75s

Example: "Planetary Assault — Atollon Sector — Seventh Fleet moving from Lothal + Lothal Garrison Corps — Thrawn commanding fleet — Gozantis carrying 1 Company of troops"
You Commit Everything in That Sector
When a Planetary Assault or Full Assault is declared, every force card in the attacking sector that the player designates is committed. You cannot selectively hold back individual ships from a fleet card during a major battle. The exception is Raids — which use only a small strike element, not the full force.

4. Action Priority
Offensive Actions Resolve First
When both players reveal their Phase 1 declarations in Phase 3, actions are resolved in the following order:

1. Offensive actions first — Planetary Assault, Full Assault, Raid, Fleet Engagement
2. Everything else second — Supply Runs, Diplomatic Missions, Intel Operations, Cell Insertions, Movements, Reinforcements

This means if the Empire declares a Planetary Assault on a sector the same turn the Rebels declared a Supply Run through it — the assault resolves first. The Supply Run fails automatically because the sector is now under attack before it can execute.
Meeting Engagement — Both Players Attack Same Sector
If both players declare offensive actions targeting the same sector in the same turn — neither side gets the attacker advantage. Both forces engage simultaneously as a Meeting Engagement.

  • Neither side can declare retreat in Turn 1 of a Meeting Engagement — you walked into each other
  • Retreat becomes available from Turn 2 onward (the following campaign turn)
  • In Men of War both forces set up in the middle of the map moving toward each other — not attacker vs defender positions
  • No attacker/defender morale distinction — both sides use their actual morale state
5. Space Battle Rules
Space Battle Happens Before Ground Battle
If the attacking player is bringing forces to a sector that has an enemy fleet defending it, a space battle must happen first. Ground forces cannot land until the space battle is resolved.

If there is no defending fleet — the attacker wins space automatically. Ground forces land unopposed.

If the defending fleet breaks through and reaches them that is what gets filmed and recorded. 9. After the Battle
GM Post-Battle Responsibilities
After filming both the space and ground battles the GM records and updates the following in the Sector Command document:

  • Casualties — update headcount on all force cards involved
  • Ships lost — reduce individual ship counts on fleet cards
  • Sector control — change control status if the battle decided it
  • Commander casualty rolls — roll 1d6 + Command Rating modifier for every named commander present
  • Supply effects — any supply disruptions from the battle
  • Political Will — adjust based on battle outcome and casualties
Commander Casualty Roll
Roll 1d6 after every battle for each named commander present. Apply modifiers:
Roll ResultOutcome
1Killed in Action — removed permanently. Faction loses 1 PW.
2Captured — opponent gains 2 PW. Can be exchanged or rescued.
3Wounded — out 2 turns. Command Rating -1 while recovering.
4Shaken — Command Rating -1 for next battle only.
5Unharmed — no effect.
6+Heroic — faction gains +1 PW.

Battle outcome modifiers: Victory light losses +2 / Victory moderate losses +1 / Defeat managed -1 / Defeat heavy -2 / Total loss -3
10. Commander Rules
Bringing a Commander to Battle
  • Commander must already be in the same sector as the force they are leading
  • Declared in Phase 1 as part of the action declaration
  • Commander presence shifts morale state up one tier
  • Commander rolls on casualty table after any battle they participate in
  • A commander can only be in one battle per turn
Moving a Commander
  • Moving costs 1 AP — the commander's entire turn action
  • Cannot use specialty the same turn they move
  • Adjacent sector — 1 turn to arrive
  • More than one sector away — 2 turns in transit, unavailable both turns
  • Rating 5 commanders (Tarkin, Thrawn, Vader) can move any distance in 1 turn — costs 1 PW

13. Vehicle Transport & Crew Scripting
Pre-Mission Vehicle Crewing — Setup Method

To have AI units start a scenario already inside a vehicle (transport truck, half-track, AT-AT etc.), you cannot simply place them in the editor. You must crew them manually during setup. This is the confirmed method from community sources.

StepWhat To DoNotes
1Place the vehicle and crew units on the map in F3 modeMake sure vehicle and crew are assigned to the same player number. They must match or crew will commandeer / exit immediately.
2Switch to F1 mode. Press the player's numpad number to take control.NumLock must be ON. Press the numpad number matching the player (e.g. Numpad 1 for Player 1).
3Select the crew units and order them into the vehicle manuallyClick the crew, right-click the vehicle to board. Wait for them to fully enter before proceeding.
4Switch back to F3 mode and save the mission (0.mi)The crew position is now saved. The vehicle will start the scenario with crew aboard.
5Copy-paste the crewed vehicle as neededEach copy retains the crew. Use this to fill out a full transport complement without repeating the process.
Keep Troops on Transports — Emit Problem Fix
If troops keep jumping out of vehicles when the scenario starts, the vehicle's .def file may have "emit on enemy" set. The fix: assign the vehicle to one player and the troops to a different player in F3, manually board them in F1, then switch the troops back to the vehicle's player before they enter. This prevents the emit-on-enemy trigger from firing on scenario start.

14. Wave Scripting — Hidden Unit Method
The GEM editor does not have a true spawn command for pre-placing units. The correct method for wave-based reinforcements is the hidden unit method — units are pre-placed on the map but tagged as inactive/hidden. Triggers remove the hidden state to activate each wave. This solves the memory crash problem by keeping all units loaded but only activating them in sequence as earlier units die and free memory.
How It Works
StepWhat To DoNotes
1Place ALL units for all waves on the map in F3Place Wave 2 and Wave 3 units at the same spawn point as Wave 1 — south edge, near DX-9s.
2Tag Wave 2 units with their wave tag (e.g. IMP_CPY2) AND set them as hidden/inactiveIn F3 entity properties, set state to inactive. They will be invisible and non-functional until triggered.
3Tag Wave 3 units similarlySame process — inactive state, their own wave tag.
4Create a trigger with an entities condition counting Wave 1 alive unitsWhen Wave 1 drops to 10% alive, fire the trigger.
5Commands: actor_state removes hidden, sets assault mode, actor_to_waypoint sends them to attackAdd a delay of 10-15 seconds before activating — simulates Gozantis landing.

15. Company Parent Tag System
When scripting wave triggers that need to count casualties across an entire company, a single tag covers all units in the company. Each unit carries multiple tags — one for its specific platoon/squad role, and one shared company-wide tag used for counting. This is required because the entities condition can only check one tag at a time — it cannot add up PLT1 + PLT2 + PLT3 + PLT4 counts automatically.

For rebel forces, use unit-type tags instead of platoon tags. Rebels are irregular forces so platoon-level structure isn't needed. One tag per unit type covers all scripting needs:
